<h2>When to Convert Images to WebP</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Web performance:</strong> Replace JPEG/PNG assets on websites to reduce page weight and improve Core Web Vitals scores.</li>
  <li><strong>Email campaigns:</strong> Smaller images load faster in email clients that support WebP.</li>
  <li><strong>App development:</strong> Use WebP in mobile and web apps to reduce asset bundle size.</li>
  <li><strong>CDN cost reduction:</strong> Smaller files mean less egress bandwidth billed by your CDN.</li>
</ul>

<h2>WebP vs JPEG vs PNG</h2>
<p>
  WebP supports both lossy and lossless compression, as well as transparency (like PNG) and animation (like GIF).
  For photographic content, WebP lossy at quality 80 typically matches JPEG quality 90 at roughly half the file size.
  For graphics with transparency, WebP lossless beats PNG by 20–30% on average.
</p>
