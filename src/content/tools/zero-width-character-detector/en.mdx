<h2>Zero-Width Character Detector</h2>

<p>
  Invisible Unicode characters silently embed themselves in text from copy-paste, AI-generated content, 
  and supply-chain attacks. They don't render visually but can carry watermarks, manipulate text flow, 
  hide malicious code, or interfere with parsing. This tool detects and removes them.
</p>

<p>
  Paste text and the detector will highlight every invisible character, categorize it, and offer 
  a cleaned version. Use strip mode to remove all categories at once or selectively — zero-width only, 
  Bidi controls only, AI watermarks (tag characters) only, or variation selectors only.
</p>

<h2>What It Detects</h2>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Zero-width spaces</strong> (U+200B) — invisible space for soft-wrapping without line breaks</li>
  <li><strong>Zero-width joiner / non-joiner</strong> (U+200D, U+200C) — join or prevent joining of adjacent characters in scripts like Arabic and Devanagari</li>
  <li><strong>Byte-order mark</strong> (U+FEFF) — marks file encoding endianness; often corrupts plain-text imports</li>
  <li><strong>Word joiner</strong> (U+2060) — prevents line breaks without rendering space</li>
  <li><strong>Bidirectional controls</strong> (U+200E-200F, U+202A-202E, U+2066-2069) — override text direction for RTL/LTR display; used in Trojan-Source attacks to hide code</li>
  <li><strong>Soft hyphen</strong> (U+00AD) — suggests line-break point when text reflows; can corrupt copy-pasted content</li>
  <li><strong>Variation selectors</strong> (U+FE00-FE0F, U+E0100-E01EF) — select glyph variants for emoji and CJK characters; accumulate when exporting from design tools</li>
  <li><strong>Tag characters</strong> (U+E0000-E007F) — reserved for invisible metadata; used for AI model watermarks and authentication tokens</li>
</ul>

<h2>How to Use</h2>

</ol>
