<h2>How to Use</h2>
<ol>
  <li>Enter the network name (<strong>SSID</strong>) — exactly as it appears in your Wi-Fi settings, including capitalization and spaces.</li>
  <li>Enter the <strong>password</strong>. Click the eye icon to reveal it for verification.</li>
  <li>Pick the <strong>encryption</strong> type your router uses (WPA covers WPA / WPA2 / WPA3).</li>
  <li>Optionally tick <strong>Hidden network</strong> if your SSID is not broadcast.</li>
  <li>Click <strong>Generate QR Code</strong>, then scan it with any phone camera to test.</li>
  <li>Click <strong>Download PNG</strong> to save it for printing or sharing.</li>
</ol>

<h2>The Wi-Fi QR String Format</h2>

<p>Wi-Fi QR codes follow a standard plain-text format that smartphone cameras parse natively:</p>

<pre><code>WIFI:T:WPA;S:&lt;ssid&gt;;P:&lt;password&gt;;;</code></pre>

<ul>
  <li><code>T</code> — encryption type (<code>WPA</code>, <code>WEP</code>, or <code>nopass</code>)</li>
  <li><code>S</code> — SSID (network name)</li>
  <li><code>P</code> — password (omit when <code>T</code> is <code>nopass</code>)</li>
  <li><code>H:true</code> — optional flag for hidden networks</li>
  <li><code>;;</code> — closing terminator</li>
</ul>

<p>Special characters (<code>{'\\'}</code>, <code>;</code>, <code>,</code>, <code>{'"'}</code>, <code>:</code>) are auto-escaped with a leading backslash so passwords containing punctuation work correctly.</p>
