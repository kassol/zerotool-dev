<h2>What is UUID v4?</h2>
<p>
  Version 4 UUIDs are generated using random numbers. With 122 bits of randomness,
  the probability of a collision is negligible — you'd need to generate over a billion
  UUIDs per second for thousands of years to have a 50% chance of a single collision.
</p>
<p>
  UUIDs are used as primary keys in databases, identifiers in distributed systems,
  file names, session IDs, and anywhere a globally unique label is needed.
</p>
