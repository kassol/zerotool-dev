<h2>How to Use</h2>
<ol>
  <li>Choose a <strong>User-agent</strong> from the dropdown (<code>*</code> targets all bots) or type a custom bot name.</li>
  <li>Click <strong>+ Allow</strong> or <strong>+ Disallow</strong> to add path rules.</li>
  <li>Optionally add more User-agent blocks for per-bot configuration.</li>
  <li>Enter your <strong>Sitemap URL</strong> if you have one.</li>
  <li>The robots.txt output updates in real time — click <strong>Copy</strong> to copy it.</li>
</ol>

<h2>Common robots.txt Patterns</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Block all bots:</strong> <code>User-agent: &#42;</code> + <code>Disallow: /</code></li>
  <li><strong>Allow all bots:</strong> <code>User-agent: &#42;</code> + <code>Disallow:</code> (empty value)</li>
  <li><strong>Block a specific directory:</strong> <code>Disallow: /admin/</code></li>
  <li><strong>Allow Googlebot only:</strong> Block <code>&#42;</code>, then add a separate Googlebot block with <code>Disallow:</code></li>
</ul>
<p>
  After generating your file, upload it to your web server root so it is accessible at
  <code>https://yourdomain.com/robots.txt</code>.
</p>
