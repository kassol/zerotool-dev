<h2>How to Use</h2>
<ol>
  <li>Paste your XML into the input panel on the left.</li>
  <li>Click <strong>Format</strong> to beautify or <strong>Minify</strong> to compress.</li>
  <li>Choose your preferred indentation from the options.</li>
  <li>Click <strong>Copy</strong> on the output panel to copy the result.</li>
</ol>

<h2>Worked Examples</h2>
<h3>Before formatting</h3>

```xml
<root><user id="1"><name>Alice</name><email>alice@example.com</email></user></root>
```

<h3>After formatting</h3>

```xml
<root>
  <user id="1">
    <name>Alice</name>
    <email>alice@example.com</email>
  </user>
</root>
```

<h2>XML Formatting Best Practices</h2>
<p>Properly formatted XML improves readability and makes it easier to spot structural issues. Use consistent indentation, keep attributes on the same line as the element when short, and always validate your XML before sending it to APIs or configuration files.</p>
