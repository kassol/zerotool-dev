  - question: "How do I export a HAR from my browser?"

  - question: "Does my HAR get uploaded?"

  - question: "What do the six waterfall colors mean?"
    answer: "They follow Chrome DevTools standard phases mapped onto HAR 1.2 spec timings: Queued/Stalled (blocked), DNS Lookup (dns), Initial Connection (connect — already includes ssl per spec), Request Sent (send), Waiting/TTFB (wait), and Content Download (receive). A field equal to -1 means the phase did not occur and is skipped."
  - question: "Why does entry.time sometimes differ from the sum of timings?"
    answer: "Per HAR 1.2 spec entry.time must equal blocked + dns + connect + send + wait + receive (summing only non-negative fields; ssl is excluded because it is already counted inside connect). If they differ, the HAR was emitted by a non-compliant tool. ZeroTool tolerates a sub-2ms float delta and still renders the request."

<h2>How to Use</h2>
<ol>
  <li>Open your browser DevTools, go to the Network tab, and export the session as a <strong>.har</strong> file.</li>
  <li>Drop the file into the dropzone (or click to choose). Parsing happens locally in your browser.</li>
  <li>Switch between <strong>Overview</strong> for stats, <strong>Waterfall</strong> for timing, <strong>Detail</strong> for headers/cookies/body, and <strong>Filter &amp; Export</strong> to slice and download.</li>
  <li>Click any waterfall row to jump straight into its Detail view.</li>
  <li>Use <strong>Reset</strong> to clear the HAR from memory.</li>
</ol>

<h2>Waterfall Phases</h2>
<p>The waterfall draws six segments per request, mapped from HAR 1.2 <code>timings</code>:</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Queued / Stalled</strong> — <code>blocked</code></li>
  <li><strong>DNS Lookup</strong> — <code>dns</code></li>
  <li><strong>Initial Connection</strong> — <code>connect</code> (already includes <code>ssl</code> per HAR 1.2)</li>
  <li><strong>Request Sent</strong> — <code>send</code></li>
  <li><strong>Waiting (TTFB)</strong> — <code>wait</code></li>
  <li><strong>Content Download</strong> — <code>receive</code></li>
</ul>

<h2>Privacy</h2>
<p>HAR files routinely contain Authorization headers, Cookie headers, response bodies, and other sensitive material. Uploading them to a hosted analyzer is risky. ZeroTool runs the parser in your browser using <code>FileReader</code> and <code>JSON.parse</code>; nothing is uploaded and nothing is persisted across reloads.</p>
