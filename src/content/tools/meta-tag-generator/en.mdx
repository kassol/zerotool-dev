faqItems:
  - question: "What's the difference between Open Graph and Twitter Card?"
    answer: "Open Graph (og:) is the social-preview protocol Facebook introduced; LinkedIn, Slack, Discord, iMessage, and most platforms now read it. Twitter (X) added its own twitter:* tags with card-type controls. When both are present, Twitter prefers its own tags and falls back to Open Graph for missing fields, so it is normal practice to ship both."
  - question: "What size should the og:image be?"
    answer: "1200x630 px (1.91:1) is the safe default for Facebook, LinkedIn, and Discord. Twitter's summary_large_image expects 2:1, and 1200x600 works fine. Keep the file under 5 MB and use PNG or JPEG. Most platforms also respect og:image:width and og:image:height when serving the preview."
  - question: "Do I need both Open Graph and Schema.org JSON-LD?"
    answer: "They serve different consumers. Open Graph powers social-platform unfurl previews. Schema.org JSON-LD powers Google rich results and crawler understanding. Their overlap (title, description, image) is small. If you want both rich snippets in search and rich previews on social, ship both — they cost almost nothing extra."
  - question: "Does this tool upload my image?"
    answer: "No. The image URL is only inserted as a string into the generated meta block and used as an img src reference inside the preview. There is no server fetch, no upload, nothing leaves your browser."
  - question: "Why does canonical URL matter for Open Graph?"
    answer: "Canonical URL is shared as og:url. Without it, social platforms guess from the request URL — which often differs (utm parameters, trailing slash, www vs apex). A wrong canonical fragments share counts and breaks deduplication when the same article is reachable through multiple URLs."

<h2>How to Use</h2>
<ol>
  <li>Fill in <strong>Title</strong>, <strong>Description</strong>, and <strong>Canonical URL</strong> — the three load-bearing fields.</li>
  <li>Add an <strong>og:image URL</strong> for proper social previews. 1200x630 PNG or JPEG works everywhere.</li>
  <li>Pick a <strong>Twitter card type</strong> — <code>summary_large_image</code> for posts with a hero image, <code>summary</code> for compact unfurls.</li>
  <li>Optionally choose a <strong>Schema.org @type</strong> to emit a minimal JSON-LD block.</li>
  <li>Switch tabs in the preview pane to verify how the page unfurls on Google SERP, Facebook, Twitter (X), and Discord.</li>
  <li>Copy the generated HTML and paste it inside your page's <code>&lt;head&gt;</code>.</li>
</ol>

<h2>What It Generates</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Basic SEO meta</strong> — <code>title</code>, <code>description</code>, <code>canonical</code>, <code>author</code>, <code>keywords</code>, <code>theme-color</code>, <code>viewport</code>, <code>robots</code>.</li>
  <li><strong>Open Graph</strong> — <code>og:type</code>, <code>og:title</code>, <code>og:description</code>, <code>og:url</code>, <code>og:site_name</code>, <code>og:image</code> (with width / height / alt), <code>og:locale</code>.</li>
  <li><strong>Twitter Card</strong> — <code>twitter:card</code>, <code>twitter:site</code>, <code>twitter:creator</code>, <code>twitter:title</code>, <code>twitter:description</code>, <code>twitter:image</code>.</li>
  <li><strong>Schema.org JSON-LD</strong> — minimal <code>WebPage</code>, <code>Article</code>, <code>Product</code>, or <code>Organization</code> block (name / description / url / image).</li>
</ul>

<p>The generated block goes inside <code>&lt;head&gt;</code>, ideally near the top — after <code>&lt;meta charset&gt;</code> and before any third-party scripts. Order does not matter for crawlers, but keeping social meta together makes audits easy.</p>
