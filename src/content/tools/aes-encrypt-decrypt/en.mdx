<h2>How to Use</h2>
<ol>
  <li>Enter your <strong>Password</strong> in the key field.</li>
  <li>To <strong>Encrypt</strong>: paste your plaintext in the left panel and click <strong>Encrypt</strong>. The base64-encoded ciphertext appears on the right.</li>
  <li>To <strong>Decrypt</strong>: paste the base64 ciphertext in the left panel and click <strong>Decrypt</strong>. The plaintext appears on the right.</li>
  <li>Click <strong>Copy</strong> to copy the result.</li>
</ol>

<h2>Security Notes</h2>
<p>AES-256-GCM is computationally secure against brute-force attacks. However, the encryption is only as strong as your password. Use a long, random passphrase for sensitive data. The authentication tag in GCM means decryption will fail loudly if the ciphertext is tampered with.</p>

<h2>What Is AES?</h2>
<p>AES (Advanced Encryption Standard) is the symmetric encryption algorithm standardized by NIST in 2001. It secures HTTPS traffic, encrypted filesystems, and password managers worldwide. AES-256 uses a 256-bit key and 14 rounds of transformation per block.</p>
